Nagaland

Dzukou Valley

It is nature’s irony that a lush green valley—rich with a variety of flora, gurgling streams, ample rainfall and large tracts of flatland—should be completely inhospitable to cultivation and therefore civilization. That’s Dzukou valley in Nagaland for you. The native Angami tribe laboured in vain for years to farm the land but finally gave up, christening it Dzukou, which in the local dialect means dull and soulless.

Assam

Forest Man of India

India has had its share of passionate environmentalists but few can match the single-minded focus and perseverance of Jadav Molai Payeng. Born in Assam in 1963 into a poor family with 13 siblings, a young Molai was distraught when he returned to his village after his school examinations–dead snakes were everywhere and cattle refused to come out of the ponds that gave them respite from the intense heat caused by an absence of any tree cover. Jadav consulted the village elders and was advised to plant bamboo trees. He took to the task in all earnest. When the forest department launched an afforestation drive in the area, Jadav joined that party as a daily wage labourer and did not stop planting more trees even after the formal initiative had ended.

Assam

Kaziranga

Kaziranga national park in Assam is home to two-thirds of the world’s population of the Great One- Horned Rhinoceros and has been a World Heritage Site since 1985. Not as well known is the fact that it is also home to the highest density of tigers in a protected area in the world. Sightings though, are rare due to the presence of dense vegetation and a number of water bodies in the park.

Arunachal Pradesh

Treks of Menchuka

Much before roads came to this part of Arunachal Pradesh, villagers traveling from Menchuka to Dorjeeling or vice-versa had to cross the Menchuka La, la meaning pass in the local language. The trail still exists and is a great short trek with some spectacular views—collages of brown and green hills with backdrops of snow-clad peaks and deep blue rivers snaking through the valleys below.

MEGHALAYA

Archery in the Abode of Clouds

Meghalaya is home to three major tribes, Khasi, Jayantia and Garo. Archery is the traditional sport of the Khasis. What makes the sport special is that there is an elaborate prelude to the archery—invoking the blessings of Ka Mei Hukum or the Mother Teacher who apparently teaches her worshippers from within, setting up of covenants or terms of challenge between two teams, arguing over these covenants and finally submitting the arguments to Ka-Mei-Hukum for her judgment.
