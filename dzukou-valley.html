Dzukou Valley

It is nature’s irony that a lush green valley—rich with a variety of flora, gurgling streams, ample rainfall and large tracts of flatland—should be completely inhospitable to cultivation and therefore civilization. That’s Dzukou valley in Nagaland for you. The native Angami tribe laboured in vain for years to farm the land but finally gave up, christening it Dzukou, which in the local dialect means dull and soulless.

Read more

Despite its unwillingness to let humans inhabit it, Dzukou valley is stunningly beautiful. A search on the internet throws up descriptions like ‘untouched paradise’ and ‘a piece of heaven on earth’, among others. With its rolling green hills, blue mountains and exotic flowers, Dzukou does meet every expectation.

Straddling the north eastern states of Nagaland and Manipur, Dzukou is easily accessible. The better route is through Kohima, the capital of Nagaland, because the trail on this side is easier and devoid of leeches. You begin with a steep climb through a forest which leads you to a clearance atop a hill. Catch your breath at a small shack while you enjoy views of mountains and valleys stretching all the way to the horizon. From here on, it’s a comfortable two hour walk along the rolling hills to the night halt at a couple of large unfurnished dormitories with a kitchen. We can also pitch tents further down the hill, if you so choose.

While a day trip to Dzukou is doable, we recommend an overnight stay, especially during the winters. Though temperatures dip as evening falls, going well below zero at night, it is worth the effort simply for the spectacular view of a million stars lighting up the night sky. The peace and solitude is overwhelming and tempts you to stay up late, but be warned for you don’t want to miss the equally amazing morning views. Dawn paints the sky in hues of crimson and orange. An off-white of settled frost replaces the green of the day before. A huge rock in the far distance and a large cross on the hill behind you add to the other-worldly aura of this place. As the sun becomes brighter, the frost turns into a low-hanging cloud of mist over the hills. Visit Dzukou and you’ll find every reason to thank nature’s irony.
