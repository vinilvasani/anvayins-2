Arunachal Pradesh

Menchuka La

Much before roads came to this part of Arunachal Pradesh, villagers traveling from Menchuka to Dorjeeling or vice-versa had to cross the Menchuka La, la meaning pass in the local language. The trail still exists and is a great short trek with some spectacular views—collages of brown and green hills with backdrops of snow-clad peaks and deep blue rivers snaking through the valleys below.

Read more

The gradient is moderately steep and an averagely fit person will probably take an hour to cross over. Unless you choose to take a detour to the neighbouring peak on which big white letters of concrete spell MENCHUKA, much like the iconic HOLLYWOOD sign.

Our recommended itinerary is to drive or ride to Dorjeeling from Menchuka and spend the morning visiting the local gompa which houses a large Buddha statue. It is tended to by an old caretaker and his wife who are more than happy to regale you with some enchanting stories. Thereafter, have a quick lunch at a pretty spot beside the Phuj river, trek across Menchuka La and climb down the other side to your waiting car or bicycle that will take you back to your homestay.
