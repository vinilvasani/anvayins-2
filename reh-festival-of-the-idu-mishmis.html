Reh Festival of the Idu Mishmis

One of the two major festivals of the Idu Mishmi tribe of north-eastern Arunachal Pradesh, Reh is an occasion for seeking the blessings of the primary God and Goddess of the tribe– Maselo Zinu and Nani Intaya. More of a family festival till well into the 60s, especially for the more affluent households, it transitioned into a community festival only at the encouragement of Ita Pulu, one of the most influential leaders tribe of the tribe.

Tribes

Every village big enough to have a reco, or community hall, houses the festival there. The biggest Reh celebration takes place at Roing, a major town in the Mishmi hills region. The reco ground is decked with colourful prayer flags and neat rows of stalls sell local art, craft, handloom and food. Igus, Mishmi priests, chant prayers in a typical Mishmi house that’s built for the occasion, and men and women of all ages dressed in bright traditional costumes mill around the hall enjoying the festivities.

History

Conveniently for those on tight schedules, the Reh has a fixed calendar–it starts on February 1 every year. While the festival lasts for six days, each day dedicated to a specific ritual, the first two are the most absorbing for a visitor. Animated chanting by the Igus, folk songs and dances, fashion shows in traditional as well as contemporary attire, exhibits of age-old methods of weaving and basket making; there’s a lot on display.

Festivals

You could also travel a short distance away to one of the nearby villages to get a peek into their celebrations. These functions are more informal and rustic and not as well choreographed as the one at Roing. It is humbling to hear the announcements and the applause for contributions made by individual households towards the celebration–a sackful of rice, a pig, a can of oil and so on. With this simplicity comes a genuine warmth towards visitors. Be prepared to be treated royally as they announce your arrival on the microphone, usher you in excitedly and serve you apong, the locally brewed millet beer, along with some fish or pork rice packed in banana leaves.

The Idu Mishmis regard their women with respect. This respect and support from the tribe has enabled their women to accomplish some amazing feats. To name a few; Tine Mena is the first woman from the northeastern region of India to scale Mt Everest, Muri Essomi Linggi achieved the same as a mother of four and Nuri Elapa has received national awards for her contribution towards women and child development. The community is proud of their achievements and the Reh 2019 celebrations were in their honour as the function was organised and performed exclusively by women and even the chanting Igus were all women.

Experiences

While you’re in the region, you could also check out some of the great treks on offer. If you’re on a tight schedule, a short trek down Mayodiya Hill is time-efficient yet exciting. For those with more relaxed schedules, we recommend the Inthu Valley trek which takes you to remote habitations in the heart of the Mishmi Hills.
