Meghalaya

Archery in the Abode of Clouds

Meghalaya, which literally means ‘abode of clouds’, is among the more popular travel destinations of northeastern India. As you walk amidst the rolling hills, deep gorges and vast green meadows of this region, it seems that time has come to a standstill. This land is blessed with unique natural formations–the gushing waterfalls of Noh Kah Likai and Krang Suri, the crystal clear river at Dauki and the living root bridges at Nongriat.

Heritage

Meghalaya is fast becoming famous for the multiple caving experiences it offers–some are plain touristy while others are for the more adventurous. Caving at Mawmluh is a unique experience for those who are reasonably fit as it involves trekking through limestone and sandstone deposits, wading through calcium- filled water and crawling through narrow spaces between giant rocks. Take a fossil trek where you see centuries-old imprints of various species. Meghalaya is home to three major tribes, Khasi, Jayantia and Garo. Archery is the traditional sport of the Khasis. What makes the sport special is that there is an elaborate prelude to the archery—invoking the blessings of Ka Mei Hukum or the Mother Teacher who apparently teaches her worshippers from within, setting up of covenants or terms of challenge between two teams, arguing over these covenants and finally submitting the arguments to Ka-Mei-Hukum for her judgment.

The locals believe that unless a player has the blessings of the Mother, he cannot win even if he is an excellent marksman. At the end of a game, it is entertaining to watch the winning team sing a victory song mocking the opponent. While the official archery competition takes place every year on April 4th, we can organise a private, guided game for you and as you will agree afterwards, it is exhilarating to be able to hit the target.
